Sensor notes · Bosch BNO055

BNO055 — A Powerful 9-DOF Sensor by Bosch

The BNO055 from Bosch Sensortec is a smart 9-DOF absolute orientation sensor that fuses accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer data onboard — so you get ready-to-use orientation without writing Kalman filters or quaternion math yourself.

What’s inside: 9 degrees of freedom

The BNO055 internally combines three motion sensors:

  • 3-axis accelerometer
  • 3-axis gyroscope
  • 3-axis magnetometer

Unlike a raw IMU, the chip runs Bosch’s BSX3 fusion algorithm and exposes high-level motion data directly over the bus.

Communication: I²C and UART

The BNO055 supports two interfaces:

  • I²C — default mode; widely supported by Arduino, ESP32, and STM32 libraries.
  • UART (TX/RX) — useful when the I²C bus is crowded or for longer cable runs.

Key outputs you can read

  • Euler angles — roll, pitch, yaw (degrees)
  • Quaternions — compact orientation for robotics and VR
  • Gravity vector — pure gravity direction
  • Linear acceleration — motion with gravity removed
  • Calibration status — gyro, accelerometer, and magnetometer
  • Internal temperature

Onboard ARM Cortex-M0

The BNO055 is not just a sensor die — it includes an onboard ARM Cortex-M0 that runs BSX3 in real time. That MCU collects and filters raw sensor data, performs fusion, and streams orientation results to your host microcontroller.

Operating modes

Mode selection depends on your environment and what you need to measure:

  • NDOF — fusion of all 9 axes; best for full orientation outdoors.
  • IMUPLUS — accelerometer + gyro only; useful indoors where magnetometer drift is an issue.
  • ACCONLY, GYROONLY, MAGONLY — raw sensor readings when you want direct access.

Modes can be changed in firmware as your application requirements change.

Where it fits

The BNO055 is a strong fit for posture-tracking wearables, balancing robots, drone attitude, and biomedical gait analysis — anywhere you need stable orientation without implementing fusion on the host MCU.
